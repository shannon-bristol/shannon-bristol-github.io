Genre

sans-serif
Modern-Humanist: the very first kind of Roman typeface. The letters are derived from a pen held at a consistent angle, creating a steady, readable rhythm. This is the style of type most commonly used for books and other longform text, so they make good paragraph fonts.

Summary

Verdana was made for people to be able to read text when it is very small on a computer monitor. It can be a pretty big and bold font so it should not be used when there will be a lot of text. It is recommended to be used on low-resolution ouput devices so it can make your document legible. The letters and numbers are taller and are more spread out. It has a resemblance to other humanist sans-serifs.
